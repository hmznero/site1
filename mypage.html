Berserk is a dark fantasy manga series created by Kentaro Miura. It follows the story of Guts, a lone mercenary with a tragic past, and his relentless struggle for survival in a brutal, unforgiving world. Known for its intense violence, complex characters, and profound themes of despair, camaraderie, and ambition, the series delves deep into human nature and existential challenges. HISTORY OF BERSERK

Guts was born from the corpse of his dead mother, hanging lifeless from a tree, and was discovered by mercenaries. He was raised by a brutal mercenary named Gambino, who treated him harshly. Guts childhood was defined by suffering. He was trained as a swordsman from a young age and endured trauma,
including abuse and abandonment. After killing Gambino in self-defense, Guts became a wandering mercenary, battling to survive in a cruel world.

Guts grew close to the members of the Band of the Hawk, especially Griffith and Casca. He admired Griffiths ambition to rule
his own kingdom and fought loyally under him. Over time, Guts began to question his role as Griffith subordinate. He wanted to forge his own path and be Griffiths equal, not just a tool in his dream.

ly rose to prominence due to Griffith’s leadership and tactical genius.

Griffith saw Guts as someone special—his “right hand” and the key to his success. Their bond became central to Griffith’s plans, and he believed Guts belonged to him. Griffith’s obsession with hi
